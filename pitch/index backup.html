The Problem

Health data is everywhere. Usable guidance is nowhere.

Millions of people manage symptoms, lab results, food choices, routines, and records across completely disconnected tools — with no way to turn any of it into clear next steps.

01

Fragmented context

Patient portals, PDFs, food trackers, notes apps — all siloed. No tool unifies them into a coherent picture.

02

Generic AI fails health

ChatGPT has no memory of your body. No context of your records. No ability to save plans and follow through.

03

Trust and privacy unresolved

77% of AI-health users are concerned about privacy. The opening exists for a purpose-built, privacy-first product.

04

Point solutions are too narrow

Symptom checkers, meal planners, and med reminders each solve one slice. No one owns the full workflow.

The Solution

A personal health workspace. Not just a chatbot.

Chat on the left. A generated, saveable artifact on the right. Every conversation becomes a plan, explanation, or routine — one that gets smarter as you add context.

The chat is the interface. The moat is the context graph — profile, documents, extracted providers, and eventually mobile health signals.

How It Works

Context in. Artifacts out. Better every session.

A bounded, governed architecture — not open-ended AI. Three defined skills, a six-question intake, and a preview panel that converts conversation into persistent, reusable outputs.

01

02
Skill Router

Trust, Safety & Compliance

Compliance is part of the product.

Claims create obligations. The strategy is: wellness-first, clinically conservative, compliance-aware, and progressively validated.

Risk mitigations — now

Wellness-first positioning
FDA enforcement discretion for self-management software without specific treatment suggestions
Bounded skill architecture
3 defined skills prevent diagnostic drift. Consistent with WHO and NIST guidance.
Privacy-by-design
Explicit consent, GDPR-aligned, minimal mobile permissions (HealthKit / Health Connect)
Safety layer & escalation logic
Hard-coded crisis pathways, red-flag escalation, confidence-aware UI, refusal policies
Auditability
Log prompt version, model version, source context, output category per generation

Regulatory landscape

🇺🇸 United States
FDA enforcement discretion for lower-risk self-management software. FTC Health Breach Notification Rule applies. GoodRx enforcement = real risk for ad-based health data use.
🇪🇺 European Union
AI Act in force Aug 2024, broadly applicable Aug 2026. MDR/IVDR classification by intended purpose. EHDS in force March 2025. GDPR: health data is special category.
Medical-data interpretation skill stays educational
Lab explanation in plain language · question prep for clinical visits · care navigation when red flags appear — never diagnosis.
